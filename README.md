# Customer Shopping Behaviour Analysis

## Overview
This is an end-to-end Data Analytics project. Explored customer shopping behaviour using transactional data from 3,900 purchases across multiple product categories. The objective was to uncover patterns in customer spending, product preferences, subscription behaviour, and purchasing habits to support data-driven business decisions.
Followed a complete analytics workflow involving data cleaning and feature engineering in Python, business analysis using SQL, and interactive dashboard creation in Power BI.

## Problem Statement
Retail businesses generate large volumes of customer transaction data, but extracting meaningful insights from this information can be challenging. This project aims to analyze customer demographics, purchasing behaviour, and product performance to identify opportunities for improving customer retention, revenue growth, and marketing effectiveness.

## Key Business Insights:

### 1. Subscribers Generated Higher Revenue
**Finding:** Customers enrolled in subscription programs contributed significantly to overall revenue and showed stronger purchasing consistency.
**Why it matters:** Subscription programs increase customer retention and lifetime value, making them a valuable long-term growth strategy.


### 2. Customer Revenue Varies Across Age Groups
**Finding:** Certain age groups contributed a disproportionately larger share of total revenue compared to others.
**Why it matters:** Businesses can focus marketing campaigns and personalized offers on the most profitable customer segments.


### 3. Repeat Customers Drive Business Growth
**Finding:** Returning and loyal customers accounted for a substantial portion of purchases and revenue.
**Why it matters:** Customer retention initiatives often provide higher returns than acquiring new customers and can significantly improve profitability.


### 4. Discounts Influence Purchasing Behaviour
**Finding:** Several products showed a high percentage of discounted purchases, indicating that customers were more likely to buy them when promotions were available.
**Why it matters:** While discounts can boost sales volume, excessive dependence on discounts may reduce profit margins and create pricing sensitivity.


### 5. Top-Rated Products Represent Growth Opportunities
**Finding:** Certain products consistently received high customer ratings and positive reviews.
**Why it matters:** Promoting highly rated products can improve customer satisfaction, increase conversion rates, and strengthen brand perception.


### 6. Shipping Preferences Impact Customer Spending
**Finding:** Customers using faster shipping options tended to demonstrate different spending patterns compared to standard shipping users.
**Why it matters:** Understanding shipping preferences can help optimize logistics strategies and improve customer experience.



## Business Recommendations
* Expand subscriber-exclusive offers to increase customer retention.
* Implement loyalty programs to convert returning customers into loyal customers.
* Review discount strategies to balance sales growth and profitability.
* Promote top-rated products through targeted marketing campaigns.
* Focus customer acquisition efforts on high-revenue demographic segments.
* Optimize shipping options based on customer preferences and purchasing behaviour.
